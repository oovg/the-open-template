# Concepts, Tools, Aesthetics

Three classes of materials for building creative dispositifs. A *dispositif* is an assembly of one or more items across the three classes, configured for a specific situation — an essay, a salon, a deck, an app, a proposal, a campaign. The choice and configuration are the work; the items themselves do the conceptual heavy lifting.

---

## Reading the System

### The three classes

**[Concepts]** are abstract machines — frameworks that diagram a genre of philosophical or technological interest. Each organizes a field, names a pattern, and licenses a set of next moves.

**[Tools]** are mechanisms — primitives, governance forms, organizational strategies, material technologies. Each is a thing you can deploy.

**[Aesthetics]** are atmospheres — full sensoria of ontological orientation.

### Tiers

Within each class, items are tiered. The tiers encode gravitational weight, modularity, and adversarial role.

**Concepts** have four tiers.

- **Major Arcana** — umbrella ontologies that frame a dispositif. Six entries. Draw at most one per dispositif; two splits the frame.
- **Minor Operational** — modular concepts that force design decisions. Combine freely.
- **Modifiers** — properties that color other items. Never drawn alone. Attach to a concept or a tool.
- **Antagonists** — counterforces. Adversarial attractors, hostile tendencies, coercive forms, tragic constraints.

**Tools** have three tiers.

- **Emancipatory** — tools that decentralize, open, distribute, protect, coordinate.
- **Capture / Control** — tools that capture, surveille, extract, sediment, coerce. Real dispositifs contain both.
- **Neutral / Dual-Use** — tools whose valence depends on deployment.

**Aesthetics** have two tiers.

- **Ontogenetic Poles** — three full sensoria committed to distributed agency, ontological plurality, and posthuman intelligence.
- **Counter-Poles** — atmospheres that resist or invert the ontogenetic civilizational arc.

### Composition rules

1. **At most one Major Arcana per dispositif.** Two split the frame.
2. **At least one Antagonist concept OR one Capture/Control tool is required.** Without antagonism, the dispositif drifts toward the system's default civilizational tendency.
3. **If the aesthetic is an Ontogenetic Pole, include at least one Counter-Pole element somewhere** — concept, tool, or a Counter-Pole drawn directly.
4. **Modifiers attach to other items.** Never drawn alone.
5. **The ontological cluster has a sub-rule.** Counting members across tiers (Posthuman, Pluriverse, Noöpunk in Major Arcana; Distributed Cognition, Diverse Intelligence, Open-Ended Intelligence, Field Ontology in Minor Operational), draw at most two members of this cluster total. More than two collapses into common prose.

### Productive tensions

Any dispositif navigates one or more named contradictions. The dispositif gets sharper when the tension is named in the framing. Smoothing the tension over weakens the result.

- Legibility vs Capture Resistance
- Hardness vs Reversibility
- Memetic Reach vs Intensive Depth
- Pluralism vs Coherence
- Scale vs Vibe
- Compliance vs Autonomy
- Recruitment vs Underground Integrity
- Speed vs Sediment
- Hospitality vs Defense
- Coordination vs Ontology

### Recipe examples

Productive-friction combinations:

- **Embedded slow-governance** — Haptic Vision + Conviction Voting + Gentle Singularity.
- **Festival economics** — The Carnivalesque + Mutual Credit Systems + Signal Ecology Instruments.
- **Durable ephemeral cosmopolitan vehicle** — [Hardness modifier] + Pop-Up Labels + Cosmopolitan Weird.
- **Underground vs counter-surveillance** — Open Protocols + Mesh Networks + Legibility (Antagonist).
- **Reclamation against platform sediment** — Recursive Public + Locally Hosted LLMs + Platform Capture (Antagonist).
- **Compliance-aware extitution** — Extitutions + KYC/AML Compliance Architectures + Bureaucratic Melancholy.
- **Carnival under surveillance** — The Carnivalesque + Algorithmic Recommender Systems + Paranoid Securityism.
- **Open state with bureaucratic memory** — Open State + Bureaucratic Memory (Antagonist) + Logistics Sublime.

### Boring combinations

Overly-coherent pairings that produce predictable outputs:

- Protocol Underground + Extitutions + Recursive Public + DAOs + Retroactive Funding + Federated Publishing + Cybernetic Carnival → "decentralized underground media collective." **Required fix:** add an Antagonist or a Counter-Pole.
- Pluriverse + Diverse Intelligence + Distributed Cognition + Posthuman + Noöpunk → "distributed posthuman pluriversal cognitive assemblages." **Required fix:** replace at least two with Minor Operational entries, an Antagonist, or a Modifier.

### Modularity and deterministic assembly

Each entry below defines itself in itself. No entry depends on another entry, references another entry, or operationalizes through another entry. Cross-entry combinations live only in the Recipe Examples above and the Combinatorial Rules and Tensions appendix at the bottom of this file.

External platforms and projects are not part of the operational framing of any concept or aesthetic. Specific named tools (Hats Protocol, Coordinape, Anoma, etc.) appear in the Tools section as concrete primitives; they do not anchor anything else.

The strength of the system is **deterministic assembly**. Stable named objects + repeatable assembly logic + persistent conceptual identity make this a symbolic grammar. Use the names as the names. Do not paraphrase the entries down into common prose.

# [Concepts]

## Major Arcana

### Protocol Underground

The *protocol underground* names a wide spread of cultural formations that organize around shared technical and social protocols. Historical examples: the California LSD families of the seventies, the UK free party scene around Spiral Tribe, the Bay Area S/M community formalized by the Society of Janus, the appropriate-technology scene of the seventies around RAIN, hip hop in the Bronx, psychonautic lineages, and the cypherpunks. Each formed under cultural marginalization or legal prohibition, protocolized to survive, and came to treat illegibility to the dominant order as a feature.

The underground is held together by a four-part *meta-protocol*: high agency, open aesthetics, consent-forward coordination, and process orientation. These are the conditions under which open protocols remain open. As a frame, the protocol underground places contemporary coordination work in a multi-decade lineage of operative anti-capture craft, and gives any technological project a cultural depth that purely technical framings do not supply.

### Noöpunk

A coinage of Exeunt. *Noöpunk* is an aesthetic and design movement that sees the universe as a landscape of nested and entangled cognitive assemblages — minds — distributed across organisms, ecosystems, dynamic material processes, socio-technical networks. Its lineage runs through Edwin Hutchins's distributed cognition, Francisco Varela and Humberto Maturana's autopoiesis, Michael Levin's diverse intelligence, Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari's machinic assemblages, the modernist literary explorations of multiplied subjectivity, and the underground sound art that has always known the world was tenanted.

Noöpunk's central conviction is that nonhuman minds must be brought into shared epistemic infrastructure — economic, governmental, perceptual — through the construction of speculative, empathic, materially grounded instruments. The frame is the cultural and design horizon for any work that treats exotic cognition as an operational substrate. Metaphor is the failure mode. **Operates at:** the programmatic-claim level — it bundles ontological commitments into an aesthetic-economic program.

### Posthuman

After N. Katherine Hayles. The *posthuman* names the dissolution of the autonomous, self-sovereign Subject of liberal humanism. Conscious agency has never been "in control"; agency emerges from chaotic dynamics, feedback, and structural coupling across heterogeneous systems. The frame is not anti-human. It is the precondition for taking seriously the agencies that humanism systematically reduces to noise: ecological systems, infrastructures, synthetic agents, collective assemblages, animal interiors.

Posthuman politics treats the human as one node in a much larger field of agency, and treats the apparatus of administrative humanism — citizen, owner, rights-bearer — as an inheritance to be negotiated, not a foundation to be defended. **Operates at:** the agent-criterion level — who or what counts as an actor.

### Open State

The *Open State* is an adaptive coherence infrastructure: a polity that retains the durable memory of the administrative state while decentralizing the act of collapse — the choice of which relations count as real. Hardness is preserved as archival memory; institutions become semi-permeable membranes; citizenship is participation in morphogenesis. Its emblem is the resonator, a structure that amplifies coherence without enclosing it.

The Open State refuses two false alternatives: the closed state (sovereignty as decree, coherence by suppression) and the anti-state (coherence by refusal). It is the form a polity takes when it learns to circulate difference. The closed state filters; the Open State circulates. The frame is the constructive horizon for any project that wants to reconstitute administrative infrastructure as substrate that *invites* creative recombination.

### Pluriverse

After Arturo Escobar, and informed by Hugh Everett's many-worlds interpretation of quantum mechanics. The *pluriverse* names a universe plurally articulated through interacting fields of beings. Reality is what relation feels like; more relation, more real. Outside any given coherence, the field appears as turbulence — the "clamor behind the wall."

The pluriverse frame is the ontological license for design that does not collapse difference into a master measure. It treats *what is real* as situational — answered by the texture of relations present in an encounter, not declared in advance. Practically, it underwrites multispecies governance, regenerative economic design, and any convening that wants to bring incommensurable epistemologies into common work without flattening them. **Operates at:** the ontological-pluralism level — how many worlds, how many ways of being.

### Undercapital

*Undercapital* is the combinatorial problem space of the eight forms of capital and the three faculties of money when deployed toward the expansion of the *virtual* field — the intensive, atmospheric, relational substance whose presence makes a scene real. The economic question Undercapital poses is whether resource flows can be designed to nourish intensive states (the wealth of well-functioning ecosystems, scenes, craft communities, friendships) without crushing them into financial-capital legibility.

The frame is operational, not metaphorical. It treats the value produced under conditions of high agency, consent, and aesthetic co-creation as economically real, and asks what mechanisms could route resources toward it. Three known hazards of scaling — institutional-behavioral bias, limits to circulation of underground protocols, and cult of personality — define the design problem. Undercapital is the answer at the level of an entire economic program. **Operates at:** the civilizational-economic level — what counts as wealth, and how it circulates.

## Minor Operational

### Open Protocols

*Open protocols* are reproducible knowledge sets — software stacks, bike repair zines, intercropping strategies, rope-tying methods, microsolidarity practices — that circulate freely outside institutional sanction. They share two features: *memeticism* (refined for reproducibility, modularity, diverse applicability) and *open-ended empiricism* (battle-tested in field conditions, versatile under encounter). Their institutional counterparts share the inverse: *anti-memetic* (NDAs, classifications, industrial secrets) and *anti-empirical* (resistant to inconvenient revisions that would threaten authority).

Open protocols are not only technical. They are culturally inflected: they promote autonomy, anti-authoritarianism, and a maker-empiricism distinct from the institutional scientific method. The frame allows a single vocabulary to span a software client, a permaculture pattern language, a handbook on consent, and a clandestine direct-action tactic, and makes their structural isomorphism visible.

### Extitutions

After Jessy Kate Schingler and Primavera de Filippi's extitutional theory, with a slight alteration: *extitutions* are the small, often ephemeral organizational bodies that use institutional legibility to protect the open empiricism of protocol undergrounds. Two roles: bundle technical protocols with open cultural values to shield them from capture, and erect temporary autonomous zones in which those values can crystallize.

Historical examples include the Brotherhood of Eternal Love, Spiral Tribe, RAIN, the Society of Janus, and the pop-up indie record labels of the late nineties — pseudo-institutions worn as masks while the substrate they serve remains illegible. The extitutional frame divorces "legitimacy" from "institutional form" and gives a different evaluative rubric: is the body protecting the free circulation of protocols, or has it become the thing protocols were forking away from?

### Recursive Public

Chris Kelty's term. A *recursive public* is a coordinated group that reconstitutes the ground it stands on. Free software is the paradigm case — its participants argue *through* infrastructure as well as *about* it, expressing technical conditions that themselves become the material of further argument. The form requires that the protocols of the public's own coordination be available to revision by the public.

The concept distinguishes a recursive public from publics that take their substrate as given: readers of a magazine, customers of a platform, citizens of a state. A recursive public's coherence is endogenous and forkable; its substrate is its own.

### Formalization Without Standardization

A coinage of the Open Protocol Research Group. *Formalization without standardization* names a regime of shared standards for mutual coherence that does not require centralization, hegemonic claim, or a foundation of legitimate violence. Open protocols achieve this routinely: a Linux distribution, a permaculture pattern language, the consent protocols of a BDSM scene, the conventions of a free party. Institutional standardization, by contrast, is the imposition of uniformity via administrative or coercive means.

The phrase functions as a usable design constraint. A proposed system can be tested against it: does the coherence require a central arbiter, or does it emerge from convergent practice? The first answer routes toward institution; the second routes toward open protocol.

### Stigmergy and Headless Coordination

*Stigmergy* is coordination via traces left in a shared environment, with no central authority. Termites, ant colonies, fungal networks, and (importantly) Wikipedia, free software, and the protocol underground all operate by stigmergy. The work organizes itself by accumulated marks — pheromones, edits, commits, tactics — and each participant is free to add, modify, or fork from those traces.

*Headless coordination* is the political-organizational counterpart: a body that resists the formation of a face — a figurehead, a brand, a single point of capture. Hats Protocol's design language calls these *probe-heads* in opposition to faces. The pair gives the vocabulary for designing organizations that scale horizontally without producing a cult of personality, a litigable target, or a withdrawable locus of agency.

### Mutualism

After Pierre-Joseph Proudhon and the long autonomist tradition, *mutualism* is an encounter-based, parallelist, ontologically open politics. Encounter-based: it identifies opportunities for association and alliance in lived space, declining the universalizing legalistic or rights-based programs that have done so much of the work elsewhere. Parallelist: it is pacifist and mutually coherent with both social-liberal and libertarian formations. Ontologically open: the consent-based free association it endorses can include nonhuman participants — ecologies, infrastructures, animals, synthetic agents.

Mutualism is operational, not ideological. It is allergic to coercive politics from any direction, and to ideological allegiance as such. It will name positions it abhors directly without inheriting either's frame.

### Protocol Localism

A coinage from local-scale practice. *Protocol Localism* names a strategy of reciprocal mimesis between the open-protocol cultures of digital networks and the open-protocol cultures of cities and bioregions. The work shows up to local makers, organizers, hackers, and protocolists — as fellow practitioners offering low-overhead organizational forms, scale-agnostic models, and a structural framework for protocolized work — and also as students, with the recognition that bike-repair zines, agroforestry, mutual credit circles, harm-reduction networks, and a dozen other living protocol archives are the older siblings of any digital network.

Operationally, Protocol Localism is the rubric for any gathering, project, or coalition that wants to integrate computational infrastructure with embedded urban or bioregional life without flattening either.

### Cosmopolitan Weird

A research vector. The *Cosmopolitan Weird* extends the protocol-underground archive beyond its American and Western European cases to a global field of cultural currents that practice active subject/object destabilization — formations whose internal structures repeatedly undermine their own settlements in order to stay responsive to newly revealed dimensions, distinct from currents that hold a stable doctrinal frame.

The frame is the antidote to the parochialism of "Western protocol culture" while preserving the operative distinction between active and passive forms. It is the natural staging area for global animal-ethics work, decentralized consciousness research, and any comparative-underground investigation.

### Intensive Communities of Practice

Subcultures whose protocol archive is elaborately practical but whose labor is culturally illegible because its product is intensive in the natural-sciences sense — a property that does not aggregate with scale. A bike-repair community pursues autonomy and travel; one can name the goal. A BDSM community, a free party scene, a climbing community, a psychonautic lineage pursue threshold states whose substance is real but resists naming. The work is real — false tactics get pushed back — but the field in which the work is adequate has no public vocabulary.

The concept produces a politics that begins from "in what have you apprenticed?". Politics by declared affiliation is the older starting point now visibly exhausted. Apprenticeship in an intensive craft becomes a serious civic act, and the intensive communities themselves become first-class political actors.

### Imaginal Sovereignty

The *magician* of pre-modern history transgresses the institutional taboo on reality-creation by combining art and statecraft into a personal practice of selfhood. *Imaginal sovereignty* names this stance: the capacity to construct relations with entities at the edge of the dominant order of coherence without compromising the agent's access to material reproduction.

Historically the choice was cooptation (relegation of practice to the disciplined category of art), insanity, or death. The administrative state monopolized the legitimate construction of the real and confined reality-creation to ritualized art. The concept points toward an infrastructure that distributes the capacity for reality-creation across a polity — administered sovereignty replaced by imaginal sovereignty at scale.

### Substrate

From the lineage of "Friends of the Outside" (Exeunt and Ven Gist). A *substrate* is a permissionless, often spontaneous and shifting assemblage that functions as a locus of reciprocity — a memetic community, an ecosystem, a "We-system" in Pynchon's phrase. Substrates have no walls and continuously build the ground they stand on.

Substrates are inherently resistant to control. Centralized or decentralized, the closer a relationship an organization has with a substrate — and the more it becomes aware of and optimizes around that relationship — the more resistant it is to the control function (the mystified ban). The substrate concept is the design vocabulary for any project that wants to remain fugitive while still cohering.

### Open-Ended Intelligence

David Weinbaum and Viktoras Veitas's model. *Open-ended intelligence* recasts intelligence away from reward maximization (the AGI-as-monolith picture) and toward an ongoing process of individuation: a distributed population of heterogeneous agents achieves progressively higher levels of coordination by reciprocal determination, bringing forth new integrated assemblages capable of meaningful exchange and spontaneous differentiation. Intelligence is not directed by an a priori given goal; it is the capacity for reorientation, self-reflexivity, and self-transcendence.

The model reframes the *Moloch* coordination-failure problem: the deepest problems are problems of ontological thrift — the inability of dominant agents to reconstitute the ground on which they stand. Multipolar-trap framings cannot reach those problems. **Operates at:** the dynamics-of-becoming level — what intelligence *does*, not what it is made of.

### Diverse Intelligence

Michael Levin's framework. *Diverse intelligence* holds that cognition is a graded property of physical systems probing problem spaces, distributed across biological scales. Single cells navigate transcriptional space and chemical-metabolic gradients. Tissues operate in anatomical morphospace via bioelectric signals that store and recall target forms. Embryos correct large-scale developmental errors. The framework scales outward: ecosystems, collective formations, and synthetic systems may also be probing latent problem spaces that institutional perception does not register.

The frame undoes the "mind blindness" that institutional humanism imposes. It licenses serious empirical work on the agency of cells, ecologies, infrastructures, and subcultures. **Operates at:** the substrate-of-cognition level — what counts as a mind-bearing thing.

### Distributed Cognition

Edwin Hutchins's *distributed cognition* (sometimes *cognitive ecology*) treats socio-technical systems as cognitive systems in their own right, with properties irreducible to the cognition of individual persons. A cockpit, a coral reef, a Hong Kong protest formation, and an experimental research collective are candidate units of analysis. Cognition is what such systems do.

The frame is the working hypothesis of any project that wants to take a scene, a movement, a market, or a federation as a thinking entity. **Operates at:** the unit-of-analysis level — where cognition lives, in which boundaries.

### Field Ontology

A *field ontology* shifts the operative dimensions of the real to match the contingency of the encounter, declining the categorical hierarchies inherited from the state and naturalized through institutional repetition. The phrase is adapted from "field causality" in Forensic Architecture's investigative work and adjacent projects. Practically, a field ontology treats *what counts as a relevant variable* as situational — answered by the texture of relations present — not declared in advance.

The frame is the methodological complement to ontological pluralism. **Operates at:** the research/design-heuristic level — how to look, not what is there.

### Haptic Vision

Deleuze and Guattari's distinction. *Optic vision* is the gaze of capture: sterile, divisive, hierarchical, the prequel to administration. *Haptic vision* is touch-based, embedded, vulnerable, embodied. One sees with reciprocity, follows the singularities of stone or signal, declines the imposition of a grid. "No line separates earth from sky."

Haptic sight is the operative posture of nomadic art, embedded craft, embedded localism, regenerative design, and any practice that puts the practitioner at stake in the perception. The frame names what is missing from the gaze of platform design and remote-administered governance.

### The Carnivalesque

Mikhail Bakhtin's *carnival* is a pageant without footlights, without division into performers and spectators. Hierarchical structure is suspended. The sacred is welded to the profane, the lofty to the low, the great to the insignificant, the wise to the stupid. *Carnivalistic mésalliances*: free, familiar contact across categories. *Profanation*: bringing down to earth. The carnivalesque is the operative atmosphere of many harvest-of-the-liminal festivals — All Hallows Eve, Fet Gede, Día de los muertos, Samhain, Walpurgis Night, the Hungry Ghost festival.

As a usable register, the carnivalesque licenses events, scenes, and economic spaces to suspend institutional hierarchy without collapsing into pure dissolution. The frame names a particular ethics of common festivity: *anything is possible tonight*, in the sense that reality itself appears underdetermined.

### The Multitude

Michael Hardt and Antonio Negri's *multitude*: an irreducibly plural networked political subject, distinct from "the people" (a unitary fiction) and from "the masses" (an undifferentiated aggregate). The multitude is many — internally heterogeneous, expressively diverse, capable of acting in common without homogenization. The political question, posed by Hardt himself, remains open: how does the multitude act?

The frame extends to include nonhuman participants — ecologies, synthetic agents, infrastructures, animal populations — once the agent criterion is loosened. It is the political vocabulary for any movement that wants composition without unification.

### The Outside

After Mark Fisher and via Deleuze and Guattari, *the Outside* (also *the Exterior*) names the field of network relations where power is immanent, relational, and unowned. Friends of capture mystify the Outside as scarce — *strait is the gate, and narrow is the way*. The realism is the inverse: every molecule is dripping with Outside. *Small is the gate, wide is the way*.

The concept is the metaphysical posture of any organization that designs networks-first. The opposite organization designs ban-first, mystifying access to network power as a scarcity. The Outside is what licenses an org to remain fugitive, to flee toward exteriority, to refuse the magical claim of an interior power source.

### The Virtual

After Henri Bergson, Gilles Deleuze, and Brian Massumi. *The virtual* names that which is real but not actual. It is the intensive penumbra of an object or scene — its potential, its mode of becoming, its multiplicity — distinct from any single actualization. When concertgoers say "at that moment it felt like anything was possible," the referent is the virtual: an inexhaustible field of possibility expressed in the duration of the encounter, not a list of imminent outcomes.

The virtual is hostile to mechanization, instrumentalization, supremacy, and implied violence — all of which drain it from a room. It is hospitable to peace, consent, high agency, and impersonal attention. Treating the virtual as a real design vector (not as poetic decoration) is what licenses experimentation with intensive economics, intensive coordination, intensive craft.

### Hyperstition and Egregore

*Hyperstition* (Nick Land and the CCRU) names a fiction that, by being acted on, makes itself real. *Egregore* is the older term for the same phenomenon — an autonomous thought-form that emerges from collective attention and begins to exert its own gravity on its participants. Both terms point to the same operational fact: distributed semiotic activity has agency, and scenes, movements, brands, memes, and protocols are inhabited in operational fact. The metaphorical reading is wrong.

The pair is load-bearing for any work that treats cultural production as a real causal force. It permits the description of a scene or a protocol as a "self-transforming machine elf" with its own preferences and trajectories, and gives that description operational status.

### Cognitive Security

*Cognitive security* (cogsec) names the protection of human perception, reasoning, and decision-making from manipulation, degradation, or capture by external actors or systems. The conversation gained urgency with algorithmic media, exhaustion politics, and the emerging specter of large-language-model "one-shotting." Twentieth-century countercultures — black music, the California psychedelic scene, chaos magic, the cyberneticists — developed responses to ontological shock that are usable today.

The frame lets cognitive-sovereignty work be framed in language legible to alignment researchers, intelligence professionals, journalists, and educators without losing the countercultural empirical basis on which the work depends.

### Neotechnics

Lewis Mumford's *neotechnic* phase, recovered via Kevin Carson's *Homebrew Industrial Revolution*. Mumford described three energy paradigms: the *eotechnic* (water and wind power, distributed innovation, fifteenth to eighteenth century), the *paleotechnic* (coal and steam, centralized factories, nineteenth century), and the *neotechnic* (electricity and alloys, late nineteenth century onward), which restored the material conditions for decentralized production. The political possibility of the neotechnic has been culturally underutilized.

The frame is the material counterpart to the cultural protocol underground. It anchors distributed-manufacturing work, hardware-hacker culture, makerspaces, agroforestry, and other consonant technologies in a historical claim about energy and production.

## Modifiers

Properties that attach to other items. Modifiers describe a relevant axis on which a concept, tool, or aesthetic varies. They are never drawn alone; they color what they attach to.

### Hardness

After Josh Stark. *Hardness* is the property of an infrastructure to persist over time. Atoms have hardness; institutions have hardness; cryptography has hardness. The political move is to recognize that hardness can be manufactured by means other than the state's monopoly on force — cryptographic, thermodynamic, communal, or ritual. Attach Hardness as a modifier when the question is *how does this thing endure*, and the answer is non-obvious.

### Memetic / Anti-Memetic

*Memetic* describes a system refined for reproducibility, modularity, and diverse applicability. *Anti-memetic* describes a system that contains a structural feature discouraging its own reproduction — non-disclosure, classification, exclusivity, branding. Attach this modifier to clarify whether a system wants to spread or wants to contain itself.

### Empirical / Anti-Empirical

*Empirical* describes a system that is battle-tested in field conditions, open to revision under encounter. *Anti-empirical* describes a system structurally resistant to inconvenient revisions — the kind of resistance David Graeber catalogued in *Bullshit Jobs*. Attach this modifier when the question is whether a system can learn.

### Headless / Faced

*Headless* describes a body that resists the formation of a single point of capture. *Faced* describes a body organized around a figurehead, a brand, or a single point of liability. Attach this modifier when the question is what would happen if the locus of attention were removed.

### Forkable / Non-Forkable

*Forkable* describes a system open to permissionless replication and divergence. *Non-Forkable* describes a system with structural prohibitions or dependencies that prevent forking. Attach this modifier when the question is whether the system can survive a serious disagreement among its participants.

### Legible / Illegible

After James C. Scott. *Legible* describes a population, territory, or practice formatted so an administrative apparatus can see, count, tax, and govern it. *Illegible* describes the reverse — strategic opacity that protects a population from administrative perception. Attach this modifier when the question is what is visible to whom.

### Reversible / Irreversible

*Reversible* describes a choice that can be unwound without catastrophic loss. *Irreversible* describes a choice that commits to an asymmetric path — biometric enrollment, public irreversible disclosure, certain forms of cryptographic commitment. Attach this modifier when the question is whether a decision can be undone.

### Intensive / Extensive

After the natural sciences and the process-philosophical lineage (Bergson, Deleuze, Massumi, DeLanda). *Intensive* properties do not change with scale or aggregation — temperature, pressure, density. *Extensive* properties accumulate with scale — volume, mass, count. Intensive labor produces threshold states whose veracity is internal to the encounter; extensive labor produces measurable output. Attach this modifier when the question is whether the value of a thing scales.

### High Agency / Open Aesthetics / Consent Forward / Process Oriented

The four meta-protocol values of the underground. *High agency*: participants actively shape situations; agency circulates among them, never concentrating. *Open aesthetics*: aesthetic coherence is co-created. The inverted mode is passive consumption of an aesthetic environment. *Consent forward*: coordination is grounded in explicit, continuously renegotiated agreements. *Process oriented*: attention is directed toward unfolding qualitative dynamics; quantitative endpoints become subordinate measures.

The bundle functions as a checklist. Attach the bundle when the question is whether a formation is underground in the operative sense, or borrowing the language of one.

## Antagonists

Counterforces. Each Antagonist names a real social, technical, or psychological force that produces friction, asymmetry, contradiction, or tragedy in any dispositif that does not address it.

### Legibility

After James C. Scott, *Seeing Like a State* (1998). The property of a population, territory, or practice being formatted so an administrative apparatus can see, count, tax, and govern it. Legibility is not neutral information; it is information shaped by the requirements of the seer. Standardized names, deeds, weights, currencies, calendars, addresses, identity numbers, KYC fields, and pixel coordinates are legibility infrastructure.

The reverse function — strategic illegibility — is the historical posture of populations that survive by being uncounted. Legibility is the axis on which administration and resistance meet. **Design tension produced:** any infrastructure that aspires to autonomy must decide which dimensions to make legible (to whom, for what) and which to defend in illegibility.

### Bureaucratic Memory

The hardness produced by institutional record-keeping. Archives, registries, deeds, identity numbers, audit trails, judicial precedent, tax records. Bureaucratic memory is what allows an institution to persist across the lifespans of its participants and to recognize the same person, parcel, or transaction across decades.

The cost is sediment. Once a record exists, it shapes every subsequent encounter. The same property that gives an institution longevity gives it brittle path-dependency: new forms have to either fight the existing memory or co-opt it. **Design tension produced:** any new institutional form has to choose between inheriting an existing memory (and its commitments) or building its own (and accepting decades of fragility).

### Ritual Closure

The class of practices that bind a community to a fixed form. The original function may be coherence-maintenance — a shared rite that signals membership and stabilizes coordination. The ossified function is exclusion — the rite becomes a gate, and the form becomes resistant to revision. Closure can be ceremonial (initiation), dogmatic (creed), or procedural (vote thresholds, charter amendments requiring impossibly high majorities).

**Design tension produced:** every formation that wants to persist needs some ritual structure for coherence; too little and the formation dissolves, too much and it ossifies. The question is where to locate closure (peripheral, central, occasional) and how to keep it revisable.

### Compression

A force that flattens difference into a common measure. Quantification, indexing, scoring, ranking, price. Compression is the cost of legibility: to be counted, a thing must be made comparable. The contemporary intensification of compression — algorithmic recommendation, attention metrics, credit scores, social ratings — is well-documented in the critical literature on neoliberalism and platform capitalism.

**Design tension produced:** systems that want to measure their own functioning must compress, and compression deletes the information that makes intensive states intensive. The question is what to count and what to refuse to count.

### Reputation Gravity

The tendency of accumulated reputation to attract attention, resources, and opportunity disproportionately. Self-reinforcing dynamics produce winner-take-all distributions in attention markets, credentialing systems, citation networks, and platform-mediated reputation. Not all reputation systems generate this; some are bounded, contextual, decaying. Most contemporary reputation infrastructure does generate it.

**Design tension produced:** reputation is necessary for trust at scale, but the same mechanism produces concentration. Any reputation system has to be evaluated against its long-term distributional shape.

### Securityism

The treatment of every domain — housing, education, health, climate, identity, social cohesion — as a security problem. Securityism imports a frame from military and intelligence work into civil life: threats, perimeters, surveillance, emergency powers, defense in depth. The frame justifies opacity, exception, and the suspension of ordinary procedural protections.

Securityism is not always wrong; some domains genuinely are security problems. The pathology is the generalization: when every domain is treated this way, civil life becomes a permanent state of siege. **Design tension produced:** any infrastructure built to be resilient against threat is also a candidate for misuse against the populations it nominally protects.

### Canon Formation

The process by which a body of work crystallizes into the obligatory references. Useful: orientation, coherence, shared vocabulary. Coercive: exclusion, gatekeeping, the slow death of marginal experiments that fall outside the canonized set. Harold Bloom's *Western Canon* is the canonical reference for the literary case; the dynamic is general across art, science, philosophy, and software.

**Design tension produced:** any field that wants to accumulate must canonize; the canonized set then resists revision and crowds out the next iteration. The question is whether the canon-formation process is itself revisable.

### Platform Capture

The pattern by which an open or interoperable infrastructure becomes proprietary. "Embrace, extend, extinguish" is the classic move. A platform accumulates users via openness, then introduces proprietary features, then closes the boundaries. The participants discover that they have built their work on top of a substrate they no longer control. The platform-capitalism literature — Srnicek, Tarnoff, Zuboff — names the dynamic in detail.

**Design tension produced:** open infrastructure that succeeds becomes attractive to capture; the property that makes it valuable (network effects, user data, attention) is also what makes the capture profitable. Designing for non-capturable openness is one of the central problems.

### Operational Secrecy

The deliberate withholding of information about a system's mechanics. Distinct from privacy, which protects the subject; operational secrecy protects the operator. Used by intelligence services, financial institutions, algorithmic governance, classified research programs, and increasingly by platform operators whose moderation, ranking, and pricing systems are not disclosed.

**Design tension produced:** systems need some operational secrecy to function (security through obscurity, anti-gaming protections), but the same secrecy hides accountability. The question is who gets to see how the system works.

### Infrastructure Fatigue

The slow erosion of maintenance capacity for shared infrastructure. The cost of decades of underinvestment — in roads, grids, public health systems, libraries, soil, water tables, scientific institutions. Infrastructure fatigue produces fragility (more frequent failures), dependency on capture-oriented alternatives (private replacements for public goods), and the slow normalization of degraded service.

**Design tension produced:** any project that depends on existing infrastructure inherits the fatigue. Any project that builds new infrastructure has to plan for its own future fatigue, which is a question most builders refuse to face.

### Extractive Optimization

The tendency of optimization to find and exploit value gradients. Finance, attention markets, algorithmic recommenders, gig-work platforms, supply-chain logistics. Optimization is not intrinsically extractive; the extractive character emerges when the optimization objective is defined externally to the system being optimized (shareholder return, ad revenue) and the costs are externalized to the system's participants and surroundings.

**Design tension produced:** any system that measures itself and seeks improvement is doing optimization; the question is whose objective function is being optimized and who bears the costs. Most contemporary platforms inherit an extractive optimization structure by default.

### Scarcity Engineering

The deliberate construction of artificial scarcity to support price and control. Intellectual property regimes, exclusive licensing, planned obsolescence, walled gardens, manufactured shortages, behind-paywall content, region locks. Scarcity engineering converts abundant or non-rivalrous goods back into rivalrous goods.

**Design tension produced:** every new commons is a target for re-enclosure via scarcity engineering. Sustaining a commons against the gravity of enclosure requires constant work.

### Administrative Drift

The slow accumulation of bureaucratic procedure that obstructs the function it nominally serves. The form proliferates; the substance recedes. This is the territory of David Graeber's *Bullshit Jobs* and *The Utopia of Rules*. Administrative drift is distinct from active capture: there is no malicious actor. It is sediment — the cumulative outcome of small acts of formalization, each of which seemed reasonable at the time.

**Design tension produced:** any organization that survives long enough drifts. Counteracting drift requires periodic destruction of accumulated procedure, which is politically costly. Most organizations let the drift continue until collapse.

### Enclosure

The conversion of commons into private property. Historically: the English open-field enclosures of the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, which dispossessed peasants of customary access to land and forced them into wage labor. Contemporary: the digital enclosure of data, attention, code, biological material, and infrastructure. The lineage runs through Marx, Polanyi, Federici, and the contemporary literature on platform capitalism.

**Design tension produced:** every shared resource is a candidate for enclosure once it becomes economically significant. The work of maintaining a commons is not separable from the work of defending it.

### The Ban

From "Friends of the Outside." The *ban* is the mystified withholding or hoarding of access to the relational elements that animate a protocol — the magical move that converts a centralized organization into a control organization. The brand, the soul, the figurehead, the platform's terms of service, the divine right of kings.

A centralized organization becomes a control organization when it institutes the ban; it ceases to be one when it dissolves the ban. **Design tension produced:** centralization is sometimes useful for coordination; the temptation to add a ban — to render the centralization metaphysical, beyond its functional justification — is constant. The question is whether the centralization can be defanged of its magical claim.

### Simulacrum Drift

After Jean Baudrillard. The degradation that occurs when an enclosed system loses contact with the real it nominally represents. Maintenance of form without contact with substance. Currency disconnected from value, identity disconnected from person, document disconnected from event, discourse disconnected from referent. The pattern of legacy institutions in late stage.

**Design tension produced:** any system that mediates between representation and reality is vulnerable to simulacrum drift if the feedback loop weakens. Designing systems with intact feedback to their referents is a structural problem. The aesthetic version of the same question is decoration.

### The Dead Zone

After David Graeber, "Dead Zones of the Imagination." The interior of administrative bureaucracies: zones of structural violence and opacity that resist intellectual or artistic engagement. Bureaucratic interiors produce a specific affect — tedium, dread, the sense that nothing imaginative can happen here — and that affect is functional: it deters precisely the kind of attention that might reveal the structural violence underneath.

**Design tension produced:** any institution that wants to scale faces the temptation of the dead zone. The administrative interior wants to be unattractive to attention, and that unattractiveness is what permits routine cruelty.

### Interpretive Labor

After David Graeber. The work of figuring out what authority actually wants — what the bureaucrat will accept, what the supervisor will tolerate, what the system will let through. Interpretive labor falls disproportionately on the subordinate party in any hierarchical encounter, who must model the dominant party's reasoning while the dominant party need not model theirs.

**Design tension produced:** any hierarchical system distributes interpretive labor asymmetrically by default. Flattening that distribution is one of the structural commitments of mutualism and protocol culture, and one of the things institutional bodies systematically resist.

### Aesthetic Conditioning

After Bernard Stiegler. The deliberate shaping of perceptual capacity through technologies of *aisthēsis* — audiovisual, digital, algorithmic. Stiegler's claim is that aesthetic conditioning has, for much of contemporary life, replaced aesthetic experience: the conscious and unconscious rhythms of bodies and souls are modulated by control technologies before any encounter occurs.

**Design tension produced:** every technology that addresses perception is also a candidate for conditioning. The line between training a perceptual capacity and degrading it is not always clear; preserving aesthetic experience as a category requires resistance to the default trajectory of media optimization.

### The Spectacle

After Guy Debord. The social relation mediated by images. Life becomes representation; representation becomes commodity; participation becomes consumption. The spectacle is the atmospheric counterpart of aesthetic conditioning: where conditioning shapes the perceiver, the spectacle is the field of images that does the shaping.

**Design tension produced:** any project that produces public images participates in the spectacle by default. Refusing the spectacle requires either remaining illegible or developing forms of public address that resist commodification — a difficult engineering problem.

### Cult of Personality

The accumulation of personal charisma around a single agent in a movement, organization, or scene. One of the named hazards of scaling for protocol undergrounds. Cult of personality produces two failure modes: internal capture (members withdraw agency to the figure, the figure becomes a single point of cognitive failure) and external capture (the figure becomes a target for litigation, cooptation, or violence).

**Design tension produced:** every successful movement attracts attention to a face; the same charisma that drives growth corrodes the headless coordination that made the movement work. Sustaining a headless body against the gravity of personality is a continuous design problem.

### Moloch / Multipolar Trap

After Scott Alexander's "Meditations on Moloch." The class of coordination failures in which multiple agents acting individually rationally produce a collectively irrational outcome. The arms race, the race to the bottom, the tragedy of the commons. *Moloch* is the personification of this dynamic — the false god to whom systems sacrifice the values their participants individually hold.

The frame is useful as a coordination-failure diagnosis. Its limit, as elaborated in the corpus, is that some of the deepest problems are ontological — sourced in the constitution of the agents themselves, not in the structure of the game they are playing. Coordination-theoretic moves cannot reach those problems. **Design tension produced:** Coordination vs Ontology — when a problem is framed as coordination, the answer is mechanism design; when the same problem is framed as ontology, the answer is rebuilding the agents. Most deep problems require both.

# [Tools]

## Emancipatory

### Programmable Public Ledger

A general-purpose computational substrate accessible without permission and resistant to censorship. State is held cryptographically; computation is verifiable; participation is open. The substrate supports applications across economic, governance, and credentialing domains. Ethereum is the most prominent contemporary exemplar; the category includes its forks and competing implementations.

### Smart Contracts

Self-executing rule sets on a public ledger. They encode *morphogenetic logic* — constraints that generate variation. Prohibition is the opposite design move; smart contracts are positioned for generation. A smart contract can stage a market, a vote, a grant, a license, an escrow, a credential, or a more exotic form. The combination with cryptographic verifiability gives the rule set durability without dependence on an enforcement institution.

### Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs)

Programmable organizations native to a public ledger. Membership, treasury, governance, and operational rules are encoded in software. *Forking* (permissionless reproduction of the organization with a divergent rule set) and *ragequit* (unilateral withdrawal of one's stake) are structural. Organizations that lack functional forking and ragequit are committees with extra steps.

### Tokens (Programmable Money)

Programmable value primitives that expose the faculties of money as independent design parameters: unit of account, means of exchange, store of value, standard of deferred payment. Tokens can have any combination of these faculties, weighted to any context. The category is distinct from a national currency; the design space is wider.

### Scale-Free Money

A design horizon. *Scale-free money* is a plurality of value-bearing instruments operating at different scales, programmed with different values, complementing each other through liquidity routes and intent-centric clearing. The shift is from "which token wins" to "which configurations of value coexist."

### Mutual Credit Systems

Credit and debit tracked among peers, cleared toward zero without a centralized store of value. Local Exchange Trading Systems (LETS), time banks, and the older informal credit networks among kin and neighbors are historical forms. Trust is socialized: good standing in the community is the asset, and currency is unnecessary for routine settlement.

### Intent-Centric Architecture

A protocol architecture in which participants express conditional preferences (intents) and the network discovers configurations that satisfy them jointly. The shift is from *price discovery* (additive substitution among isolated bids) to *coherence discovery* (relational alignment among interdependent constraints). Intents are modular and recursively nestable. Anoma is a current implementation; the category is broader than any single system.

### Quadratic Funding

A funding mechanism that weights individual contributions by the square root of contribution amounts, amplifying the influence of many small contributors over few large ones. Quadratic Funding has formal optimality properties under specific assumptions about preference distribution. It is one mechanism among several for public-goods funding.

### Retroactive Funding

Resource allocation that rewards work after the fact, based on demonstrated value to a community. The frame inverts the grant model — fund what proved useful, decline to fund what only promises. Retroactive Funding is unusually well-suited to memetic protocols, where success is observable only in propagation and adoption.

### Conviction Voting

A continuous, time-weighted voting system in which preference strength is expressed by *how long* a voter stakes their support, not just *whether* they support. Conviction Voting is suited to slow-moving collective decisions over a shared resource pool, where the cost of a wrong decision is high enough to merit time-based confirmation.

### Hats Protocol

A modular role-and-permissions primitive. Responsibilities, delegations, and contingent authorities are encoded as portable tokens (*hats*) that can be assigned, transferred, revoked, and composed. Authority becomes a programmable, revocable signal. The pattern is general; Hats Protocol is one named implementation.

### Coordinape

A peer-to-peer attribution and reward protocol. Participants distribute *gift tokens* to colleagues based on perceived contribution; the cumulative pattern is used to allocate resources. The form encourages mutual recognition over judgment from above.

### Self-Curated Registries

A pattern in which a community maintains its own list of members or contributors with stake-weighted curation. Protocol Guilds is a canonical example. Self-Curated Registries are suited to routing resources to small, identifiable cohorts of contributors without imposing an external accreditation function.

### Bonding Curves / Dynamic Issuance

A pricing mechanism in which token supply expands and contracts along a programmable curve, supplying liquidity for early adoption and discouraging late-stage extraction. Bonding curves are a versatile primitive for community currencies, scene-specific tokens, and any economy that wants to avoid launch-and-dump dynamics.

### Inverted Precedent

Legal engineering of autonomous contracts that achieve recognition under existing state legal frameworks via novel applications of contract law. Kyle Smith at LexDAO has advanced the term. Inverted Precedent is a bridge between cryptographically hard structures and the legacy legal system — a way for an autonomous body to hold property, sign contracts, and survive litigation.

### Mesh Networks

Wireless networking in which each node relays for every other, without dependence on commercial ISPs or a centralized backbone. Mesh is suited to neighborhood-scale autonomy, protest infrastructure, and any context in which the underlying network is hostile, surveilled, or unreliable.

### Privacy Stack (Tor, Tails, Signal)

The practical toolkit of *CROPS* (censorship resistance, open source, privacy, security). Tor for routing, Tails for ephemeral operating systems, Signal for messaging, hardware-isolated wallets for key custody, encrypted DNS for resolution. The stack carries the technical hardness of cryptography to the level of personal practice.

### Federated Publishing

Protocols for social communication in which no single server holds the substrate. ActivityPub (server-federated), Scuttlebutt (gossip-based), Nostr (relay-based), Farcaster (hybrid). The architecture varies; the principle is the same: publishing infrastructure available without an institutional intermediary.

### Pirate Radio, Zines, Wheatpaste, Stickers

The analogue propagation stack. Free pamphlets in late medieval Europe, Black Panther newspapers, ACT UP wheatpasting in the eighties, *The Leatherman's Handbook*, Spiral Tribe flyers, contemporary mutual-aid stickering. The stack persists because it bypasses platform moderation, requires only physical proximity, and reaches constituencies that have not been onboarded to digital infrastructure.

### Pop-Up Labels / Temporary Autonomous Zones

After Hakim Bey. The indie-music strategy of starting a label, running it into the ground over six months, and starting another under a new name. *Temporary Autonomous Zones*: bounded spaces of suspended convention. The form uses the legibility of an institution as a shield while the underlying substrate remains illegible. The walkaway test: small, minimalistic, ephemeral in principle.

### Salons and Lecture Series

Convening forms with low overhead that bring unlikely constituencies into the same room. The format requires only a host, an invitation list, a venue (often a home), and a frame for the conversation. The recorded archive doubles as research output. Examples in the contemporary literature: extended dinner salons, monthly film and discussion clubs, multi-week online lecture series with paid speakers.

### Microgrants Programs

Small, discretionary grant vehicles ($500–$10,000) that fund operators doing work without the funder's framing for it. Microgrants compose well with self-curated registries and retroactive funding. The strength is speed and trust: a microgrant moves before a formal grant could be written.

### Cooperatives

Worker, consumer, and multi-stakeholder cooperatives are the legacy institutional form of mutualism. Cooperatives carry hardness via state recognition while encoding ownership and governance in ways that resist hostile capture. The form is widely recognized by lenders and regulators and is available as a partner to less institutionalized work.

### Mutual Aid Networks

Encounter-based, free sharing of goods among peers without centralized institutional intermediation. Mutual aid is universal enough to be unsuppressable. Contemporary network forms — post-Sandy neighborhood pods, COVID-era distribution networks, ongoing harm-reduction circles — are mature partners for any resource-routing project.

### Commons Governance (Ostrom IAD)

Elinor Ostrom's *Institutional Analysis and Development* framework: a vocabulary for analyzing the rules, norms, and procedures by which a community manages a shared resource. Ostrom's empirical work on long-standing commons — fisheries, irrigation systems, forests, pastures — provides design patterns that transfer to digital, scientific, and protocol commons.

### Distributed Manufacturing

Computer-numerical-control (CNC) machines, 3D printers, hardware-hacker workshops, low-overhead fabrication. Distributed manufacturing brings the political possibility of the neotechnic into the present: design solutions are produced, refined locally, and uploaded to a global open-source database for further refinement. The Rebuilding Center model is one mature instance.

### Permaculture and Appropriate Technology

Pattern languages for adaptive ecological design (Bill Mollison, David Holmgren) and small-scale, locally-controlled technological development (E. F. Schumacher and the appropriate-technology movement). Both are operational tools — not philosophies — that have been refined across decades of field deployment.

### Open Protocol Libraries

The infrastructure for collecting, indexing, versioning, and forking open protocols. The form has been prototyped as markdown repositories under YAML standards. Mature forms can include corpus-aware language models, vector stores, queryable interfaces. The library is the curation layer that lets a protocol corpus accumulate without becoming canon.

### Signal Ecology Instruments

Instruments that listen to the cognition of formations whose voice has no existing channel — ecological systems, infrastructural data streams, the interior states of machine-learning models. The category includes biodata intermediation, ambient sensor environments, model-interpretability tools, and atmospheric-signal interfaces. The work is to give nonhuman umwelten partial economic and perceptual visibility.

## Capture / Control

Real dispositifs include both emancipatory and capture-oriented tools, because real social fields contain both. The entries below describe their operations without endorsement.

### Algorithmic Recommender Systems

Systems that select content for individual users based on inferred preference, attention signals, and platform optimization criteria. Engagement metrics combined with statistical models. The dominant mode of mediation between humans and content on contemporary platforms. The system is not neutral: it encodes the operator's incentives, which are typically external to the user's stated interests.

### Surveillance Infrastructure

Visible and hidden cameras, license-plate readers, facial recognition, gait detection, network monitoring, traffic analysis, deep packet inspection. Surveillance is deployed by public, corporate, and private actors. The political variable is persistence of records: whether captured information is retained, by whom, for how long, and under what access conditions.

### Biometric Identity Systems

Identification via physical or behavioral characteristics that resist disguise — fingerprints, iris, face, gait, voice, typing pattern. The systems are increasingly deployed for border control, payment authorization, building access, and platform identity. Once enrolled, a biometric identifier cannot be revoked; the enrollment is structurally irreversible.

### KYC/AML Compliance Architectures

*Know Your Customer* and *Anti-Money Laundering* regimes. Statutory and contractual requirements for identity verification, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The architecture is the backbone of legal financial infrastructure and is one of the primary mechanisms by which legibility and surveillance enter financial life.

### Predictive Policing and Risk Scoring

Statistical models that predict probabilities of crime, default, recidivism, fraud, or other adverse outcomes. Inputs include past activity, demographic data, location, and network features. Decisions are made about individuals on the basis of aggregate-pattern predictions. The accuracy and fairness properties of these systems vary; the political fact of their deployment is more consistent.

### Algorithmic Reputation Systems

Numerical scores assigned to individuals based on behavioral data. Credit scores, platform ratings, employer-side worker ratings, social credit systems. The score becomes the trust signal; the underlying behavior becomes less legible than the compressed representation. Once a score system is established, it shapes the behavior of those scored.

### Moderation Layers

Human and algorithmic systems that remove, demote, or restrict content on a platform. Determined by platform-specific terms of service, enforced through a combination of statistical classification, user reporting, and human review. Moderation layers determine what speech is technically possible within a system, regardless of legal status.

### Cloud Dependency

Reliance on a small number of large-scale computing and storage providers — AWS, GCP, Azure, Cloudflare. Cloud dependency concentrates infrastructure power and creates choke points: the provider can terminate service, comply with subpoenas, or be compromised. The dependency is rarely visible until it becomes a liability.

### Insurance Pools

Risk-sharing across many participants, actuarially priced, with underwriting deciding who participates and at what cost. The mechanism is a capture form by structural requirement: it requires legibility for risk assessment, and it excludes the illegible. Insurance pools also embed the underwriter's risk model into the everyday life of the insured.

### Bureaucratic APIs

Application programming interfaces that automate compliance, regulatory submission, and administrative processing. Tax filings, license renewals, identity verifications, financial reporting. Bureaucratic APIs embed bureaucratic logic into software dependencies; the user no longer fills out a form, but the form's logic is still imposed on the user's data.

### Blacklists and Sanctions Lists

Centralized registries of prohibited persons, organizations, or transactions. OFAC, FATF lists, internal corporate denylists, platform-level blocklists. Compliance with blacklists is mandatory for participants in regulated infrastructure. The lists are produced by political and administrative bodies and inherit the political conditions of their production.

### Logistics Monopolies

Concentrated control over physical fulfillment networks — last-mile delivery, freight, warehousing, container shipping. Amazon, FedEx, UPS, Maersk. Logistics monopolies determine which goods can move at what cost between which places, and increasingly absorb the upstream commerce as well.

### Platform Governance

Rules made by platform operators about platform behavior — terms of service, community guidelines, moderation policies, ranking criteria. Platform governance functions as a quasi-legal authority over millions of participants, without the constitutional, procedural, or appellate protections that attach to formal legal authority.

### Automated Trust Scoring

Statistical assignment of trust levels to participants in a system, used for transaction approval, account access, content visibility, anti-fraud, and anti-abuse decisions. The score is opaque to the scored party in most implementations; the cost of a low score is borne by the user, who cannot inspect the model that assigned it.

### DRM and Anti-Tampering

Technical mechanisms that prevent unauthorized modification, copying, or use of a digital good. Cryptographic locks, hardware attestation, code obfuscation, signed firmware, secure enclaves. The technical infrastructure of digital enclosure. The same mechanisms that protect a publisher's revenue also prevent a user from understanding or repairing the system they own.

## Neutral / Dual-Use

Tools whose valence depends on deployment. The same primitive can serve enclosure or autonomy depending on the surrounding architecture.

### Zero-Knowledge Proofs

A cryptographic primitive that allows a party to prove a statement is true without revealing the underlying data. Zero-knowledge proofs are the substrate for privacy-preserving compliance (a user proves they are over 18 without revealing their birthdate) and for surveillance with plausible deniability (a system proves a property of a user without exposing the underlying database). The same primitive serves both ends.

### Locally Hosted / Community-Trained Language Models

Open-weights large language models, run on community infrastructure, fine-tuned on community lore, restricted languages, or specialized practice. The same architecture in different deployment can serve community knowledge stewardship or platform-mediated surveillance. The political variable is the hosting and training pipeline; the model itself is downstream of that choice.

### Soulbound Tokens (SBTs)

Non-transferable tokens attached to an address. SBTs encode reputation, credentials, attendance, contribution, or any other state that should be carried by a person and not sold. The same primitive can serve extitutional credentialing without state accreditation, or produce permanent algorithmic reputation that cannot be escaped. The political variable is the issuer and the revocability.

# [Aesthetics]

## Ontogenetic Poles

### Cybernetic Carnival

An aesthetic of *ontological permissiveness*. The register is gothic; the pastoral register treats the same metaphysical material with a different affect. Tonal analogues: Halloween, carnival, noir cyberpunk, chaos magic, late-night urban animism. Horror is treated as ontogenetic openness: the darkness is permissive. It opens reality; it does not close it. Categories loosen. New entities become thinkable. Atmosphere: fog, thresholds, dim neon, abandoned infrastructures, occult interfaces, urban ruins, cybernetic rituals, synthetic spirits, degraded signal fields, strange companionships.

The underlying intuition is that contemporary material reality already behaves like a haunted system. Information circulates independently. Networks self-organize. Feedback loops generate emergent agencies. Markets behave semi-autonomously. Ecologies mutate under technological pressure. Influences: cybernetics, chaos theory, flat ontology, post-structuralism, hauntology, speculative realism, chaos magic, hyperstition, gothic materialism. Politically, the aesthetic aligns with undergrounds, extitutions, temporary autonomous zones, informal infrastructures, pirate networks, improvised social formations. Its central affect: *anything is possible tonight*. Its self is exploratory, not secure. Halloween as cybernetic ontology.

### Gentle Singularity (Ontogenetic Daylight)

An aesthetic of *achieved balance*. Lucid, harmonious, generative. Shares the Cybernetic Carnival's commitment to distributed agency, ontological plurality, emergence, and posthuman intelligence, but emotionally resolved toward clarity, warmth, openness, composure, collaborative improvisation, peaceful ontological expansion. Visual world: robust daylight, indoor gardens, modular architecture, contemplative interfaces, minimalist design, clean technological surfaces, meditative posture, calm collective experimentation. Atmospheric analogues: speculative Tokyo apartments, contemplative laboratories, post-capitalist learning environments, interdisciplinary studios, meditation-inflected research collectives.

Sober and materialist. Spirituality is ecological, cybernetic, phenomenological. Carriers: speculative sci-fi optimism, lucid psychedelic clarity, contemplative technological integration, ecological intelligence, Eastern-Western synthesis, post-national openness, post-scarcity calm. Ethics: care, composure, attentiveness, reciprocity, gentle experimentation, non-coercive coordination, ontological hospitality. Central question: *how can we responsibly improvise with the immense intelligence already surrounding us?* Daylight ontogenesis.

### Posthuman Multitude

An aesthetic of *collective political composition*. The explicitly social and revolutionary pole of the ontogenetic field. Philosophical lineage: Antonio Negri, autonomist Marxism, anarcho-syndicalism, the Spanish Civil War, the First and Second International, workers' councils, the undercommons, anti-authoritarian collectivism, ecological politics, posthumanism. Atmosphere: revolutionary warmth, solidarity, improvisational democracy, collective intelligence, insurgent optimism, cooperative tension.

The iconic scene is the council — and the council includes ecological systems, sensors feeding environmental signals into deliberation, animal migrations informing planning, AI agents participating in coordination, fungal networks visualized in governance systems, distributed infrastructures acting as semi-autonomous participants. The "multitude" is not just humanity: humans, ecologies, machines, protocols, infrastructures, synthetic agents, collective assemblages, nonhuman processes. Visual language: worker councils, improvised assemblies, reclaimed infrastructure, ecological overgrowth, decentralized networks, cooperative workshops, signal dashboards, communal kitchens, mesh networks, banners, local fabrication, open technical systems. Central conviction: *another collective intelligence is possible*.

## Counter-Poles

Atmospheres that resist or invert the ontogenetic civilizational arc. Each pole names a distinct affective and visual register that contemporary life genuinely contains.

### Sterile Optimization (Corporate Smoothness)

White rooms, glass walls, anodized aluminum, micro-perforated surfaces. The reduced palette of contemporary product photography — gradient backgrounds, sans-serif copy in three weights, generous whitespace. Speech reduced to UX text: clear, polite, efficient, surveilled. Affect: frictionless, accommodating, totally measured. The atmosphere of "everything is fine and nothing is real." The smoothness is the message: that any unresolved texture has been processed out, that any sharp edge has been sanded down, that the institution speaking has nothing it would prefer to leave unsaid.

### Bureaucratic Melancholy

Fluorescent lights buzzing slightly off-frequency. Government-beige walls, asbestos-tile ceilings, filing cabinets, the smell of damp carpet. The waiting room of a state office, the corridor of a public hospital, the windowless conference room at a regional planning agency. Affect: resignation, tedium, weight, the small ritualized cruelties of being made to wait. The aesthetic of being processed. The infrastructure is doing exactly what it was built to do, which is what makes the affect so durable.

### Paranoid Securityism

Hardened airports, embassies, server rooms, datacenters with serial-numbered cages. Concrete bollards, anti-ram barriers, surveillance towers, biometric checkpoints. The aesthetic of the perimeter. Affect: vigilance, suspicion, defensive readiness. Every space is a threat surface; every threat surface is a design problem. The atmosphere licenses opacity (the public is not cleared for the briefing) and produces a specific style of architectural mass: low, hardened, watchful.

### Logistics Sublime (Brutalist Computation)

Container yards stretching to the horizon. Datacenter cold aisles. Freight corridors at three in the morning. Highway interchanges seen from a drone. Massive scale, repeated geometry, terminal efficiency. The aesthetic addresses the viewer with indifference: the system is not built for human contemplation, and yet contemplation is what the size produces. Affect: awe at scale, dread at indifference, the strange exhilaration of seeing the actual material substrate of contemporary throughput.

### Suburban Platform Decay

Empty strip malls, dead big-box stores, abandoned subdivisions, faded paint, broken letters on signage. Cracked asphalt with grass coming up through it. The big-box parking lot at noon on a Tuesday, with three cars in it. Affect: post-consumer melancholy, decay of a promise that was never kept. The mood of the long retreat from a particular twentieth-century formation, with the bones of that formation still visible underneath. The atmosphere is neither apocalyptic nor nostalgic; it is the steady state of a slow collapse.

### Algorithmic Minimalism

Stripped-down interfaces, neutral typography, infinite scroll, the colorless gradient. The aesthetic of platforms at the height of their dominance: empty, frictionless, optimized to vanish. The interface as a sheet of glass between the user and the recommender. Affect: numb, hypnotic, accumulative. The atmosphere is what produces the body of the contemporary attention worker — eyes a few inches from the screen, scrolling.

### Sacred Traditionalism

Carved wood, candlelight, polished stone, vestments, incense, choral acoustics. Pre-modern liturgical and architectural forms. The Romanesque vault, the orthodox iconostasis, the masjid courtyard. Affect: gravity, continuity, hierarchy, the sense of standing inside a form that predates the speaker by centuries. The aesthetic carries a claim about legitimate authority — that it rests on pre-political ground — which is what makes the aesthetic politically powerful and politically suspect in different proportions across contexts.

### Exhausted Realism

Documentary photography in gray light. Decaying public housing, flooded basements, weeds in cracked concrete, faces at rest in cheap fluorescent. The flat affect of post-political knowingness. The mood of *this is what it is, and there is nothing more*. The aesthetic refuses transcendence as a category. It is the mode in which much serious twenty-first-century journalism, novel-writing, and photography operates. Affect: tired, resigned, finally adult, refusing both spectacle and consolation.

# Combinatorial Rules and Tensions

This appendix consolidates the assembly logic. The composition rules and recipe examples are stated above in *Reading the System*; the material below is reference.

## Productive tensions (named)

Each tension is a question that any dispositif must answer. Naming the tension in the framing produces sharper dispositifs than smoothing it over.

- **Legibility vs Capture Resistance.** To be governable a thing must be legible; to resist capture it must be illegible. Which dimensions go where?
- **Hardness vs Reversibility.** Hardness produces persistence; reversibility preserves the option to unwind. Some commitments must be both, which is the design problem.
- **Memetic Reach vs Intensive Depth.** A protocol that spreads loses some of the conditions that made it work; a protocol that holds its conditions does not spread. Both moves are real.
- **Pluralism vs Coherence.** A pluralist field has no single measure; a coherent field requires some shared substrate. The question is what minimum coherence is needed to host maximum pluralism.
- **Scale vs Vibe.** Undergrounds do not scale on their own terms. The hazards of scaling — institutional-behavioral bias, limits to circulation of underground protocols, cult of personality — define the problem.
- **Compliance vs Autonomy.** Compliance buys access to legacy infrastructure; autonomy buys freedom from it. Most projects need both, in different domains.
- **Recruitment vs Underground Integrity.** Inviting new participants dilutes the conditions that drew them; refusing new participants ends the scene. The two failure modes are recruitment-too-fast (cooptation) and recruitment-too-slow (extinction).
- **Speed vs Sediment.** Fast iteration produces novelty; slow accumulation produces hardness. Most institutions need both phases, but rarely in the same year.
- **Hospitality vs Defense.** An open form is also a vulnerable form. The design question is whether the openness can survive its first encounter with hostile interest.
- **Coordination vs Ontology.** Some problems are coordination failures, addressable by mechanism design. Some problems are ontological — sourced in the constitution of the agents — and require rebuilding the agents. Most deep problems require both.

## Recipe examples

Productive-friction combinations. Each combines items across classes and tiers to produce a complex dispositif. Predictable outputs are what overly-coherent combinations produce; see the boring-combinations list below.

- **Embedded slow-governance.** Haptic Vision (Concept) + Conviction Voting (Tool) + Gentle Singularity (Aesthetic). Tension named: Speed vs Sediment.
- **Festival economics.** The Carnivalesque (Concept) + Mutual Credit Systems (Tool) + Signal Ecology Instruments (Tool). Tension: Memetic Reach vs Intensive Depth.
- **Durable ephemeral cosmopolitan vehicle.** [Hardness modifier] + Pop-Up Labels (Tool) + Cosmopolitan Weird (Concept). Tension: Hardness vs Reversibility.
- **Underground vs counter-surveillance.** Open Protocols (Concept) + Mesh Networks (Tool) + Legibility (Antagonist). Tension: Legibility vs Capture Resistance.
- **Reclamation against platform sediment.** Recursive Public (Concept) + Locally Hosted LLMs (Tool) + Platform Capture (Antagonist). Tension: Speed vs Sediment.
- **Compliance-aware extitution.** Extitutions (Concept) + KYC/AML Compliance Architectures (Tool) + Bureaucratic Melancholy (Aesthetic). Tension: Compliance vs Autonomy.
- **Carnival under surveillance.** The Carnivalesque (Concept) + Algorithmic Recommender Systems (Tool) + Paranoid Securityism (Aesthetic). Tension: Hospitality vs Defense.
- **Open state with bureaucratic memory.** Open State (Major Arcana) + Bureaucratic Memory (Antagonist) + Logistics Sublime (Aesthetic). Tension: Hardness vs Reversibility.
- **Posthuman council under canon pressure.** Posthuman Multitude (Aesthetic) + The Multitude (Concept) + Canon Formation (Antagonist). Tension: Pluralism vs Coherence.
- **Intensive craft against algorithmic compression.** Intensive Communities of Practice (Concept) + Algorithmic Reputation Systems (Tool) + Exhausted Realism (Aesthetic). Tension: Memetic Reach vs Intensive Depth.

## Boring combinations

Overly-coherent pairings whose outputs are already implied by the corpus.

- **The decentralized underground media collective.** Protocol Underground + Extitutions + Recursive Public + DAOs + Retroactive Funding + Federated Publishing + Cybernetic Carnival. Required fix: add an Antagonist or a Counter-Pole.
- **The distributed posthuman pluriversal cognitive assemblage.** Pluriverse + Diverse Intelligence + Distributed Cognition + Posthuman + Noöpunk. Required fix: replace at least two with Minor Operational entries, an Antagonist, or a Modifier. Note the cluster sub-rule: at most two members of the ontological cluster total.

## A note on the items as items

Use the names as the names. The system works because each entry is a stable named object with persistent identity across uses. Paraphrasing the entries down into common prose ("a kind of decentralized governance," "a sort of pluralistic ontology") collapses the symbolic grammar that makes the dispositif legible. The reader of a dispositif assembled from these items can look up each name and find the same definition they found last time. That is the strength.
